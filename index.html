Background & Need

Why This Program Matters

High school students often have high potential but limited exposure to practical technology, innovation-based thinking, communication skills, and career awareness in science and engineering.

Most students learn theory without understanding real-life applications. This program is designed to bridge that gap by introducing students to hands-on learning, problem-solving, and confidence-building.

Practical Technology

Students learn theory without understanding real-life applications of technology.

Innovation Thinking

Limited exposure to design methodologies that drive modern solutions.

Communication Skills

Lack of confidence in presenting ideas and communicating effectively.

Career Awareness

Insufficient awareness about careers in science, engineering, and technology.

500+ Students Impacted

Program Structure

Topics Covered

A comprehensive curriculum designed to spark curiosity and build future-ready skills across 7 core modules.

5.1 Core Module

Innovation & Design Thinking (IDT)

Understanding innovation through daily life examples and structured design frameworks that empower students to solve real problems.

A

Problem Observation & Awareness

  • Identifying problems in daily life, school, and surroundings
  • Understanding the difference between complaints and real problems
  • Encouraging curiosity and awareness
Activity

Students list common problems observed in their school or home.

B

Scientific Thinking in Daily Life

  • Understanding how everyday objects work
  • Asking "Why?" and "How?" questions
  • Linking science concepts to real life
Examples

Why does a fan rotate?  ·  Why does a bulb glow?  ·  Why does a mobile phone need charging?

C

Goal Setting & Dream Mapping

  • Understanding short-term and long-term goals
  • Importance of planning and consistency
  • Connecting education with future aspirations
Activity

Students write one personal goal and the steps to achieve it.

D

Learning from Failure

  • Understanding that mistakes are part of learning
  • Developing resilience and confidence
  • Famous examples of failures leading to success
Outcome

Students learn to face challenges without fear.

E

Design Thinking Process

→

→

  • Solving real-life school and community problems
  • Team-based idea generation
